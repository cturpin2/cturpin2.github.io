Hand Motion Controlled Mini-Mech

Background

This project aims to create a tabletop-scaled version of the Prosthesis mech suit, enabling intuitive control via a glove-based system that maps finger movements to the mech's legs. Sponsored by Exosapien Technologies, this 1:20 scale prototype uses servo motors for actuation and flex sensors for control.

Design and Approach

Mechanical Design

  • Selected micro MG90 servo motors for their torque and cost-effectiveness.
  • Used a geared joint mechanism for compact, aesthetic leg movement.
  • Designed a simplified chassis for easier 3D printing and assembly.

Glove Design

  • Flex sensors mounted on gloves track finger motion.
  • Sensors are connected to ESP32 microcontrollers for wireless control.
  • Implemented a sliding sheath design to prevent sensor distortion.

Integration and Controls

  • Used the ESP32 microcontroller to map flex sensor data to servo positions.
  • Added noise reduction through averaging and calibration routines.

Testing and Results

The prototype was tested for servo torque, noise levels, and user intuitiveness. The mech demonstrated smooth movements and reliable communication, though stability during walking remains a challenge.

Recommendations

  • Adjust chassis design to lower the center of mass for improved stability.
  • Refine the mapping between finger motion and mech leg movement.
  • Use screws instead of glue for securing servo gears in future iterations.
